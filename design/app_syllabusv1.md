## PART 1: The Foundation (Daily Deprogramming)
*Estimated Word Count: ~12,000 words (30 Daily Lessons, ~400 words each)*

This section is delivered sequentially to the user upon joining. It uses the EasyPeasy / Freedom Model framework to systematically destroy every psychological illusion keeping the user trapped.

### Week 1: The Anatomy of the Trap (Deconstructing the 'Why')
*Objective: Shift the user from thinking "I am giving up something I love" to "I am escaping a trap."*
- **Day 1: The Matrix Concept.** Introduction to the "Big Monster" (psychological brainwashing) vs the "Little Monster" (physical withdrawal). Explaining that willpower is the wrong tool.
- **Day 2: The Pitcher Plant.** Why the addiction *feels* like a choice but is actually a neurological slope. The concept of gradual dopamine down-regulation.
- **Day 3: The Deprivation Fallacy.** Destroying the fear that life will be boring without it. Introducing the idea that non-users do not feel deprived; they feel free.
- **Day 4: Stop Counting the Days.** The danger of streaks. Why tracking "Days Clean" reinforces the identity of an addict and creates the Failure Spiral.
- **Day 5: The Simulation Fallacy.** (From `gemini_talk_scenarios.md`). Why porn is an "anti-practice" that makes you worse at absolute intimacy, not a harmless outlet.
- **Day 6: The Empathy Deficit.** Understanding the reality of the industry and shifting the focus from self-pity to external empathy.
- **Day 7: First Week Audit.** Interactive review. "What is one illusion you believed last week that you no longer believe?"

### Week 2: Deconstructing the Illusions (The "Stress Relief" & "Boredom" Myths)
*Objective: Prove mathematically that the behavior holds zero intrinsic value.*
- **Day 8: The Stress Relief Myth.** Explaining how porn actually *causes* the stress it claims to relieve via dopamine crashes and increased heart rate.
- **Day 9: The Boredom Cure.** Why the behavior doesn't cure boredom; it destroys your ability to be entertained by normal life.
- **Day 10: The Concentration Fallacy.** The physical reality of brain fog. Why clearing your head by "giving in" actually causes a 48-hour dopamine hangover.
- **Day 11: The Novelty Escalation Trap.** Why vanilla isn't enough anymore. Explaining the Coolidge Effect and why escalating to 'leaks' or hardcore is a sign of a dying neurological receptor, not an expanding fetish.
- **Day 12: The 'Serious Adult' Fallacy.** (From `gemini-talk.md`). The rationalization that highly intellectual or stressed people "deserve" or "need" a crude outlet.
- **Day 13: Mis-Selling of Knowledge.** The trap of "researching" neuroscience as a form of procrastination instead of actually quitting.
- **Day 14: Second Week Audit.** Rating the subjective value of the behavior from 1-10. Goal is to logically bring it to 0.

### Week 3: Surviving the Hardware Glitch (The Physical Withdrawal)
*Objective: Recalibrate the user's relationship with the physical sensation of an urge.*
- **Day 15: Meet The Beast.** Introducing AVRT. Why the logical 'You' does not want it, but the limbic hardware does.
- **Day 16: The Pang is Weak.** Realizing that the physical withdrawal symptom is actually incredibly mild—it's just a slight empty feeling. The agony is 100% caused by *doubting* your decision.
- **Day 17: Reward Value Updating.** Introducing Mindfulness. How observing a craving without acting on it physically rewires the orbitofrontal cortex.
- **Day 18: Urge Surfing.** The exact 60-second physiological protocol to ride out a craving.
- **Day 19: The Pendulum Swing.** Avoiding the trap of swinging from active addiction into extreme, repressed purity culture/moralism. Maintaining Clinical Neutrality.
- **Day 20: The Bait-and-Switch.** Understanding the "Chaser Effect" after a slip.
- **Day 21: Third Week Audit.** Interactive physical calibration.

### Week 4: The Anchor (Building the Non-User Identity)
*Objective: Solidify long-term ACT principles. Moving from "quitting" to "living."*
- **Day 22: The Dogma Defuser.** How to handle the shame if you slip, treating it like a mechanical failure rather than a moral stain.
- **Day 23: The Moving Goalpost.** Dealing with the brain's attempt to say "Just one more time, but on these special terms."
- **Day 24: Psychological Flexibility.** Accepting that triggers will exist for a while, and that is okay. You don't have to fight them; you just don't feed them.
- **Day 25: The Value Alignment.** Writing down the core values that the "Hardware Glitch" prevents you from achieving.
- **Day 26-29: Advanced Deconstruction.** User-submitted rationalizations from the community, logically dismantled.
- **Day 30: The Final Freedom.** You are not "recovering." You are free.

## PART 2: The Shield (The Mandatory Intercept Scripts)
*Estimated Word Count: ~4,000 words (10 Scripts, ~400 words each)*

These are the immediate, AVRT-based conversational scripts triggered by the Panic Button. Each script is a decision tree.

1. **TRIGGER: "I am stressed / I need relief."**
   - *Script Flow:* Acknowledge the physical stress. Ask: "Will a dopamine crash in 10 minutes lower your cortisol, or raise it?" Force the user to admit the math doesn't work.
2. **TRIGGER: "Just a quick peek / The Novelty Trap."**
   - *Script Flow:* Call out the Beast. "This is the limbic system negotiating. Peeking is the exact mechanism that restarts the starvation loop. You are observing a hardware glitch."
3. **TRIGGER: "I'm bored."**
   - *Script Flow:* "Boredom is a lack of stimulation. You are about to fry your dopamine receptors, ensuring you will be *more* bored by regular life tomorrow."
4. **TRIGGER: "I'm too horny, I can't think."**
   - *Script Flow:* "That is the Little Monster dying. Do not fight it. Observe the tension in your stomach. It cannot force your hand to move."
5.  *... (Additional scripts for Anger, Loneliness, The 'Serious Adult', The 'Moving Goalpost', etc.)*

## PART 3: The Hacker (Urge Surfing & Somatic Observation)
*Estimated Word Count: ~1,500 words*

Detailed, guided text (and eventually audio) scripts to be used *during* a craving.
- **Script 1: The 60-Second Scan.** A clinical walkthrough of body sensations to detach identity from instinct.
- **Script 2: The Reward Prediction Error.** An interactive exercise where the user guesses how good they will feel afterward vs. how good they *actually* felt last time, exposing the brain's lie.

## PART 4: The Anchor (Post-Audit / 'Relapse' Autopsy)
*Estimated Word Count: ~2,500 words*

A clinical, judgment-free flow that happens if the user engages in the behavior.
- **Step 1: The Zero-Shame Primer.** "No moral failure occurred. A neurological loop executed based on a false belief. Let's find the bug in the code."
- **Step 2: The Rationalization Identification.** "What exact sentence did your brain say to convince you to do this?"
- **Step 3: The Deconstruction.** The app forces the user to write down why that specific sentence was a lie.
- **Step 4: The Reset.** "You are not back at day zero. The knowledge you gained is still there. Your value count remains intact. Close the loop and walk away."
