# Syllabus v2 Approaches: Breaking the Linear Model

`app_syllabusv1.md` utilized a traditional, linear "30-Day Course" model. While this is comprehensive, it asks the user to passively read content day-by-day. 

To improve engagement and retention, **Syllabus v2** can be structured using one of the following dynamic architectures:

## Approach A: The "Archetype / Persona" Path (Hyper-Personalized)
Not everyone struggles with the same illusions. A user primarily driven by the "Novelty Trap" doesn't need 5 lessons on the "Stress Myth."
- **The Flow:** The user starts with an intensive onboarding audit to determine their "Trap Archetype" (e.g., The Stressed Executive, The Lonely Escapist, The Bored Gamer, The Novelty Escalator).
- **The Syllabus:** The app dynamically generates a custom 14-day path that *only* attacks their specific illusions. If they are the "Stressed Executive," their syllabus targets the physiological reality of cortisol spikes and sleep degradation caused by dopamine crashes.
- **Why it’s better:** It feels highly tailored. Users aren't reading generic advice; they feel seen.

## Approach B: The "Skill Tree" Model (Self-Directed Progression)
Instead of forcing a linear Day 1 to Day 30 timeline, the syllabus is presented as a map of "Illusions" to be conquered.
- **The Flow:** The user sees a web or specific nodes (e.g., Node 1: "The Concentration Myth", Node 2: "The Serious Adult Fallacy"). They can click and explore whatever they are currently experiencing.
- **The Catch (The Audit):** To clear a node, they don't just read an article. They must pass a Socratic "Audit" where they prove to the app they logically understand the flaw in their thinking.
- **Why it’s better:** It respects the user's autonomy and current mental state, turning passive reading into active investigation.

## Approach C: The "Triage to Therapy" Model (Physiology First)
A brain fried by intense dopamine loops literally cannot process high-level logic (EasyPeasy) on Day 1. The Prefrontal Cortex is offline.
- **Phase 1: The ER (Days 1-7):** The syllabus provides *zero* deep philosophy. It exclusively teaches physical survival: The Physiological Sigh, Urge Surfing, Haptic Interruptions, and recognizing the physical "Beast" (AVRT). The user just learns to physically survive the neurochemical withdrawal.
- **Phase 2: The Logic (Days 8-21):** Once the dopamine receptors begin upregulating and the brain clears, the app introduces the EasyPeasy cognitive deconstruction to logically destroy the desire.
- **Phase 3: The Anchor (Days 22-Indefinite):** Focuses on Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)—building the core values for the rest of their life.
- **Why it’s better:** It matches the biological reality of dopamine receptor recovery.

## Approach D: The Socratic "Truth Engine" AI
Instead of fixed articles, the entire syllabus exists "under the hood" as a set of logical milestones. 
- **The Flow:** The user never reads an article. They open the app and converse with a specialized AI. The AI’s only job is to ask them questions that force them to accidentally dismantle their own illusions. 
- **The Syllabus:** The app tracks which "nodes" (illusions) the user has successfully logically deconstructed in conversation, unlocking new areas of focus over time.
- **Why it’s better:** "People rarely argue with their own conclusions." If the app tells them they are wrong, they get defensive. If the app asks the right questions so *they* realize they are wrong, the realization is permanent.
