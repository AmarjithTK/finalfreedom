# Micro-Swiping Architecture ("Duolingo for Deconditioning")

To convert the 113-page EasyPeasy Hackbook into 300 highly engaging "Truth Cards," the layout and psychology of the content must be completely reformatted. The user learns through **action and consequence** (swiping and revealing the truth) rather than passive reading.

## 1. Anatomy of a "Truth Card"
Every piece of information is structured as a physiological or psychological binary.
*   **Front Text:** A single bold statement, usually representing an illusion or a common belief. (Max 15 words to prevent skimming).
*   **The Action:** 
    *   `Swipe Right` = Agree ("Yes, this is true.")
    *   `Swipe Left` = Disagree ("No, this is false.")
*   **Back Text (The Reality Check):** When swiped, the card flips. It states `CORRECT` or `INCORRECT`, followed by a snappy 2-4 sentence logical deconstruction that explains the neurochemical or psychological truth.
*   **The Emotional Hook:** If they swipe wrong, they feel a slight sting of being logically "bested." This makes the subsequent lesson unforgettable.

## 2. Structure of the "Decks" (The Syllabus)
The 300 cards are divided into 15 "Decks." A Deck is equivalent to a chapter or phase in the syllabus. A user completes 1 Deck per day (~20 cards).

## 3. Example Cards in Action

Here is how the textbook format translates into the Truth Card format.

### Example A (From Deck 5: The Stress Illusion)
*   **Front:** "When I am stressed, watching porn helps me relax and brings my stress levels down."
*   *User Swipes Right (Agree).*
*   **Back (INCORRECT):** "Dopamine is an excitatory neurotransmitter. It physically raises your heart rate, constricts your blood vessels, and induces arousal. You are not relaxing. You are confusing the crash of pure exhaustion with peace."

### Example B (From Deck 3: The Little Monster)
*   **Front:** "The physical withdrawal from dopamine is painful and requires immense willpower to endure." 
*   *User Swipes Right (Agree).*
*   **Back (INCORRECT):** "The physical withdrawal is actually incredibly mild—it feels like a slight hunger or emptiness. 100% of the agony you feel is psychological, generated by you doubting your decision. The Little Monster has no teeth."

### Example C (From Deck 9: The Willpower Trap)
*   **Front:** "Using willpower to fight urges gets easier the longer your streak is."
*   *User Swipes Right (Agree).*
*   **Back (INCORRECT):** "Willpower requires you to believe you are giving up something valuable. As long as you believe it has value, you will feel deprived. You don't use willpower to avoid drinking battery acid, because you know battery acid is useless. Realize this is useless, and willpower becomes unnecessary."

### Example D (From Deck 10: The Novelty Trap)
*   **Front:** "If I escalate to more extreme material, it's just because my sexual tastes are maturing and expanding."
*   *User Swipes Right (Agree).*
*   **Back (INCORRECT):** "Your tastes aren't expanding. Your baseline dopamine receptors are dying. You require more extreme shocks to feel the same baseline level of chemical reward you used to get from a passing glance. This is mathematical tolerance, not sexual maturity."

## 4. UI/UX Specifications for Flutter Development
*   **Widget:** Use a `TinderSwapCard` or a customized `Dismissible` widget in Flutter.
*   **Haptic Feedback:** When swiping left/right, provide a light satisfying haptic pop. When the card flips over, provide a distinct haptic pattern based on `Correct` vs `Incorrect`. 
*   **Progress Bar:** A smooth, filling bar at the top representing the user's progress through the daily deck (e.g., Card 12 of 20).
*   **Completion Reward:** When a deck is finished, no confetti or "alarmist" celebrations. Just a calm, elegant fade to a screen that says: *"Illusion Dismantled. You are one step closer to baseline."*
