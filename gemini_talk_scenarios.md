The dopamine reward system in the brain operates on the "Coolidge Effect"—the biological dictate that novel reproductive opportunities yield higher reward spikes. Once a genre is saturated, the brain views it as "conquered" and ceases to release maximum dopamine. The user did not lose interest in porn; their brain simply demanded a higher dose of *extreme novelty* to achieve the baseline chemical spike it had grown accustomed to. The pivot to "leaks" is not a change in preference; it is a symptom of severe dopamine desensitization and tolerance escalation.

### 1.3 Super App Integration & Intercept Strategy
- **Module Name:** The Novelty Escalation Trap
- **Intercept Trigger:** When the user feels an urge for "just checking what's new" or looking for a specific leaked folder.
- **Active Deconstruction:** The app forces the user to log *why* they are bored of vanilla content. By actively typing out that their brain has built a chemical tolerance, the user uses their prefrontal cortex to recognize that the new "leak" is just the exact same drug in a slightly different wrapper. 
- **The "Time Machine" Reality:** The app surfaces reminders that in three months, they will be completely bored of this new "leak" genre too, and the cycle will simply demand an even more extreme baseline.
